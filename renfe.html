Renfe Ticket Booking UX Research

Website experience

Purpose

Make the process of ticket booking and travel planning convenient and easy for users.

Key Functionalities

  • Ticket Booking
  • Train Information & Schedules
  • Change/Cancel Ticket
  • Customer Service & Support

Key User Group

Travelers or commuters who use train services in Spain, such as tourists, local residents, business travelers.

Research Method

Cognitive Walkthrough Method

The method aims assess key tasks with one or more evaluators and uncovering usability issues within by using 4 critical questions:
  1. Will the user try and achieve the right outcome?
  2. Will the user notice that the correct action is available to them?
  3. Will the user associate the correct action with the outcome they expect to achieve?
  4. If the correct action is performed; will the user see that progress is being made towards their intended outcome?

Task 1

Usability

Imagine you want to go to Sevilla for the weekend. Please show how you would book tickets on the website.

Identified Usability Issue 1:

The destination is shown based on previous searches, but the search button is nowhere to be found.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Recognition Rather than Recall: The call-to-action button for ticket searching is not visible. It should be easily retrievable, not requiring users to actively look for it.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • To make it more intuitive for users, instead of hiding the complete search panel on the homepage, showing it directly upon users’ arrival would make the booking experience more efficient.
    Since the main goal of users who go to the renfe website is to book tickets, giving the search panel a bigger portion of the layout would save time for users and make the booking experience wholesome.

Identified Usability Issue 2:

When users try to book tickets after checking timetables, the price and buy option does not work.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Error Prevention: The functions shown on page do not work. With careful design, problems are more likely to be prevented.
✔

Severity rate

  • Big Problem: Users are not able to complete the task at all due to this problem.
✔

Recommendations

  • Take away the options to check price and purchase tickets, let the Timetables Page serve with one clear purpose.
    To connect users to buying tickets, put a hyperlink saying “Book Tickets” to let users know where to go and also be guided back to the homepage to book tickets.

Identified Usability Issue 3:

Following “Super Yes” to go to Black Friday Sales, arriving at a page where there is no option to search tickets, and none of the hyperlinks guide you to search/book tickets.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Recognition Rather than Recall: The call-to-action button for ticket searching is not visible. It should be easily retrievable, not requiring users to actively look for it.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • Users who clicked on “Super Yes” are expecting to find good deals, rather than more details in text, which guide them no where close to the purchase.
    Instead of hiding the complete search panelOr show estimated lowest fare prices (i.e. Skyscanner) to motivate purchases.

Task 2

Findability

You click on the recommended destinations on the website, please show how you would book tickets from there.

Identified Usability Issue 1:

After clicking on one of the recommendations, it does not lead you to ticket booking, instead, takes you to a page with details of services.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Recognition Rather than Recall: The call-to-action button for ticket searching is not visible. It should be easily retrievable, not requiring users to actively look for it.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • When users follow the recommended destination option, they’re expecting to see ticket options. The website could summarize the dates for round trip with best prices, or options to check one-way best prices. It would not only benefit the users, but also increase sales for renfe. Users can be more inclined to make purchases with guidings.

Identified Usability Issue 2:

Following the highlighted hyperlink, it leads users to an irrelevant page. The destination box on the page doesn’t show the previous searched destination option.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Match Between System and the Real World: The system should speak the users’ language, rather than following system-oriented logics. Make information appear in a natural and logical order.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • Make sure the hyperlinks on website lead users to relevant content.
    While users are doing a related series of searches on related destinations, save search history for users.
    Previously input information should be visible and retrievablewhenever appropriate.

Task 3

Findability

Imagine you booked without a renfe account and there is a change of plans. Please show me how you’d change the dates for your outward & return tickets.

Identified Usability Issue 1:

When you go back to previous page from the SEE ALL STATIONS without selecting any station from the list, all the previous input ticket information is gone, it doesn’t save user’s history.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Recognition Rather than Recall: Lost of previous input history can make users frustrated because users shouldn’t have to remember one part of the dialogue to another.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • In the same series of task, previous input information should be automatically saved when they go back from one of the tasks.
    Especially when the previously input information is very detailed and time consuming for users.

Identified Usability Issue 2:

Users aren’t able to search tickets by only the Purchase Code/Ticket Number, but the website separates the information collection into two sections.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Match Between System and the Real World: The system should speak the users’ language, make information appear in a natural and logical order. Rather than exhibiting a seemingly unnecessary step for users.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • Combine all collection of information in one page, including Purchase Code/Ticket Number, Departure, Destination, Journey Date, and E-mail.
    Since with only the Purchase Code/Ticket Number, users can’t have access to change tickets, there shouldn’t be a point of separating information collection into two sections.

Identified Usability Issue 3:

Users can not change outward and return tickets at the same time. They can only change one ticket at a time.

✔

Usability Heuristics

  • Match Between System and the Real World: The system should speak the users’ language, rather than following system-oriented logics. Make information appear in a natural and logical order.
✔

Severity rate

  • Small Problem: Makes users confused, frustrated, takes longer but eventually completes the task.
✔

Recommendations

  • Implement an option to change outward and return tickets together.
    For example, users initially bought discounted tickets and when they want to change dates, they’d also want to change both to discount ones. Checking one ticket at a time could result in knowing one ticket has a suitable date but needing to go through all that process again to check if the other ticket also has available alternatives.

Decision Making

Most usability issues identified are rated as “small” but there are none rated as “cosmetic”. Many small problems can lead to big profit loss. As there are more and more platforms arising for booking train tickets, renfe should look into these small problems before it’s too late.
Since most of the usability issues are “small” problems but since the key function of the renfe website is for Ticket Booking, thus, I would recommend renfe to prioritize problems related to it. The reason why Black Friday Sales Booking is ranked higher than general booking is because at this very moment Black Friday Sales is the main campaign renfe is promoting and has limited duration. If this issue is resolved, there is high chance profits exceed the expectations. After Ticket Booking, Travel Planning is what I think renfe should prioritize, as this function guides users to booking tickets. Lastly, is the usability issues related to Change/Cancel tickets, standing from renfe’s point of view, this would be the last tasks to consider as these don’t happen that often comparing to ticket booking experiences.
