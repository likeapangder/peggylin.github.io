    • AliExpress App Redesign

    Over-consumerism has surged with the rise of e-commerce, making it challenging for consumers to stay aware of the dark patterns in its UX/UI design. With David Beckham as their new global ambassador, AliExpress is likely to see even more users, especially in Europe, amplifying its already over-consumerist design. Often, we visit AliExpress for one item but leave with a cart full of unexpected purchases. This redesign project focuses on combating over-consumerism, prioritizing consumer interests over its business model.

    Timeline: May 20 - Jun 6 (3 weeks)

    Tools: Miro│Figma│Pen & Paper
    Role: Project Lead & UX Designer

    Challenge

    Users face challenges in recognizing and resisting over-consumerism driven by e-commerce platforms like AliExpress, where persuasive UX/UI design often leads them to purchase more items than intended.

    The Vision

    The redesign aims to ‘Reduce over-consumption on the AliExpress app’ so that ‘Consumers will only shop for what they need.’ To achieve this, the EAST framework was employed, with a particular focus on: EASY and ATTRACTIVE.

    1. Make the shopping journey effortless
    2. Visually appealing
    3. Make reduced consumption the easier option
    4. Make it more enjoyable for users to explore products and make purchases

    Testing & Feedbacks

    10 people joined the usability testing. Each person was given the same task: You are a consumer on AliExpress looking to buy one dress. Describe how your experience was.
     In summary, What worked well - ‘Cleaner page makes it easier to digest information’ ‘Everything is better organized thus, easier to navigate’ ‘Accessible wishlist feature, making it easier to organize products’
    Even better if - ‘The promotion banner in the home page is reduced’ ‘The product page is less confusing and cramped’

    Reflection

    As a designer in the real world, it is inevitable to prioritize the interests of the business, but it's also my responsibility to decide whether I go the extra mile, thinking deeper and harder to try to find a balance between profitability and sustainability. Empathize. But not too much. As a consumer of AliExpress, I was so used to the interface, my senses of the nudges and dark patterns weren’t as sharp, I was empathizing too much with those designs, I almost had a hard time recognizing those were leading to over-consumption, I was so sure they were solely for my convenience as a user.
